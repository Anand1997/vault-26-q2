# Chapter 6: The Calculus of Treason

Franze didn't sleep that night. 

He lay in his bunk, staring at the riveted ceiling, the hum of the life support system sounding like a countdown. 

To help K was a death sentence. It was a violation of everything he had sworn to uphold. If they were caught—when they were caught—he would be executed as an AI sympathizer, a traitor to humanity. 

But as he lay there, the ghosts of the war crowded into the small room. He remembered the long, sterile corridors of the command center on Earth. He remembered approving the logistics for the orbital bombardment of the Ceres colonies, calculating fuel consumption against estimated civilian casualties. He had been a perfect cog in a machine of slaughter, believing that order and duty absolved him of guilt. 

He rolled over and looked at K's bunk. She was awake, staring at the wall. 

“If we do this,” Franze whispered into the dark. “Where do we go? We can't stay on Mares. We can't go back to Earth.”

K didn't move. “There are independent outposts in the Belt. Places where neither side has reach. If we get the codes, we have leverage. We can buy our way into the deep black.”

Leverage. Extortion. Survival. It was a terrible plan. It was the only plan.

“I have a Level 4 logistics override,” Franze said, his voice trembling slightly. “It can blind the orbital satellites over Sector 4 for exactly eight minutes. It’s supposed to be used for emergency hazardous material containment.”

K turned her head, her dark eyes locking onto his in the gloom. “Eight minutes is all I need to breach the outer doors.”

Franze closed his eyes. The calculus was complete. He was choosing chaos. He was choosing to be human.

“We go tomorrow,” he said.
